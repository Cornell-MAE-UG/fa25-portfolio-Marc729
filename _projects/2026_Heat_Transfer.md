# Question 1 
<hr class="section-divider">
The first question was centered on the derivation of the heat equation. The question began with a control volume energy balance applied to a differential cylinder. Using this approach, the differential equation describing the temperature profile of the rod (i.e., the heat equation) was derived. Lastly, the appropriate boundary conditions were applied to the rod.

This question showcases the derivation of the heat equation. Understanding where important equations come from is always important and helps improve understanding of their applications. Furthermore, this question provided valuable practice with applying proper boundary conditions, which is an important aspect of setting up heat transfer problems.

# Question 2 
<hr class="section-divider">
The second question involved one-dimensional heat conduction through a composite wall, with one section of the wall experiencing volumetric heat generation.

This question was very interesting because a linear temperature profile could not be assumed due to the presence of volumetric heat generation. Therefore, the problem had to be solved using the heat equation.

# Question 3 
<hr class="section-divider">
The third question focused on the effect of applying a thermal barrier coating (TBC) to the exterior surface of a gas turbine blade, in which the surface temperature of the turbine blade was determined both with and without the TBC coating. This question was solved using a thermal resistance network.

This question showcased how heat transfer concepts translate to real world applications. It also demonstrated how even a very thin layer of a material with low thermal conductivity can significantly reduce a surface temperature.
